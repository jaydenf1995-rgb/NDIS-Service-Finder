Understanding Your NDIS Plan

Published November 19, 2025

Learn how to make the most of your NDIS plan and understand your funding categories.

What is an NDIS Plan?

Your NDIS plan is your personalized roadmap to getting the support you need. It outlines your goals, the funding you've been allocated, and the types of supports you can access to live an ordinary life.

Key Components of Your Plan

  • Core Supports: For everyday activities like personal care, community access, and consumables
  • Capacity Building: To develop skills and independence through therapies, training, and employment support
  • Capital Supports: For equipment, home modifications, or assistive technology

Making the Most of Your Funding

Work closely with your support coordinator to ensure your funding is used effectively. Regular plan reviews help adjust your support as your needs and goals evolve over time.

Quick Tips for Success:

  • Keep track of your budget using the NDIS participant portal
  • Communicate regularly with your providers about your goals
  • Request plan reviews if your circumstances significantly change
  • Keep records of all supports and expenditures

How to Choose the Right Support Worker

Published November 19, 2025

Tips for finding support workers that match your needs and personality.

Finding the Right Match

Choosing a support worker is one of the most important decisions you'll make. The right person can significantly improve your quality of life and help you achieve your goals.

Key Considerations

  • Personality Fit: Do you feel comfortable with them?
  • Experience & Skills: Do they have experience with your specific needs?
  • Availability: Can they work the hours you need?
  • References: Always check previous client feedback
  • Qualifications: Verify their certifications and training

The Interview Process

Conduct informal interviews to assess compatibility. Ask about their experience, approach to support, and how they handle challenging situations. Trust your instincts - if something doesn't feel right, keep looking.

Trial Period

Start with a trial period to ensure the relationship works for both parties. Regular check-ins during the first few weeks can help address any concerns early.

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • Unwillingness to provide references
  • Poor communication during the hiring process
  • Vague answers about experience or qualifications
  • Unprofessional behavior or attitude

Navigating SIL Accommodation

Published November 19, 2025

A guide to Supported Independent Living and finding the right accommodation.

What is SIL?

Supported Independent Living (SIL) is funding for support and/or supervision of daily tasks to help you live as independently as possible in your own home.

Types of SIL Arrangements

  • Individual Living: Living alone with support workers visiting
  • Shared Living: Living with other NDIS participants
  • Specialist Disability Accommodation (SDA): Purpose-built accessible housing

Finding the Right SIL Provider

Research providers thoroughly. Consider their reputation, staff qualifications, and whether their values align with yours. Visit potential homes and speak with current residents if possible.

SIL Funding Process

  1. Get assessed for SIL in your NDIS planning meeting
  2. Obtain quotes from potential providers
  3. Submit quotes to the NDIS for approval
  4. Sign a service agreement with your chosen provider
  5. Regularly review your arrangement and support needs

Questions to Ask Providers:

  • What is your staff-to-resident ratio?
  • How do you handle emergencies after hours?
  • What activities and community access do you support?
  • How are support workers matched to residents?
  • What is your process for handling complaints?
